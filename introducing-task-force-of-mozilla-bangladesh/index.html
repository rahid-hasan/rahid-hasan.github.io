Introducing Task Force of Mozilla Bangladesh

Standard

Mozilla Bangladesh working as an active community in Bangladesh for last few years. It was started with limited number of peoples & with their hard works. Day by day it passed a quite great journey. In these times we arranged lots of events. Lots of people started contributing & become Mozillians. These journey was never quite smooth though but when we look back we feel it was quite amazing! Now we have 3100+ Facebook group members, 1500+ Facebook page followers & 1700+ Twitter followers. Believe it or not we have had a 10x boost from last year! Its quite impressive, isn’t it?

Apart from this Mozilla re-branded Student Reps to Firefox Student Ambassadors on April 2013. It helped us to get more contributors around the region. Now most of the campus has FSA presence. And a significant number of Firefox Clubs formed. We got hundreds of FSA in the region. May be they all are not active, but I believe most of them are or at least trying to do something. They all fighting for the better web. How amazing that can be?

With the giant boost of the community we faced some problem to keep it organized. And it become too hard to keep track their activities. As most of the active contributors are city based, so decentralization of the community is getting barred. And sometimes it was observed that a few contributors are trying to be lone wolf. Its also hampered the community coherence. So we started planning how this booming community can be more active, organized & united. At the end of the November 2013 I started an thread “Empowering Community with Effective Mechanism” in our community mailing list. We discussed for how we can formed some contribution area specific task force. The basic idea of the task force was simple. We have active contributors for most of the contribution area. These contributors will be lead by the task forces. The task force create & manage a set of contributors with their individual interest & capabilities. We believe this is a good step to manage the community with the speed of the recent community boost. Each task force will have a ‘Lead’. And a limited number of ‘Peer’. All other active & passionate contributors identified by the task force will be ‘Member’ of that task force. Important thing is the purpose is not to identify the contributors but guide the contributors & create more.

In the mid of December we asked the self nomination for Lead & Peer for 8 task forces: 1. Community Engagement, 2. WebDev, 3. L10n, 4. Marketing, 5. Apps Development, 6. Webmaker, 7. Student Ambassadors & 8. Pr & Communication. The Community IT & WoMoz task force will be launched later as there was not enough contributors. The response was great, more than expected. We got 28 contributors interested to be a Lead/Peer of a task force. All of them disclosed their ideas & views by answering 4 questions:

  1. Please briefly describe your experience with Mozilla Bangladesh (or any other open source / IT community).
  2. Why you think you are a leader of this community?
  3. What will be your goal for this task force?
  4. Where do you want to see Mozilla Bangladesh in 2014?

After the deadline of nomination a team of volunteers (Mahay Alam Khan, Ashickur Rahman, Shafiul Azam Chowdhury & Rahid Hasan) started analyzing the inputs. Based on the inputs in the nomination pad, previous records, past contribution, working area & able to manage time this team of volunteer proposed the task force Lead/Peer. In the beginning of January 2014 the proposal was submitted to a set of most active community members & leaders of MozBD for final discussion & selection. After all of this steps & discussions we are now able to conclude the task force selection.

Each task force will be consist of Lead, Peer(s) & Members. We are just announcing the Lead & Peer(s) of the task forces. Members will be added by the individual task force based on the interest, passion & necessity. And it should be informed to all other task forces & larger community. A task force member can become a Peer (if needed) after the discussion with all other task force Lead & Peer with appropriate reasons. Each task force will be expired & reformed in each six months. As it is the first time & quite experimental, the first task force will be expired in December 2014.
